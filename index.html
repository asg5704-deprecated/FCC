Chronological timeline of Nikola Tesla

  • 1856 - July 10th, born during a thunderstorm in Austria-Hungary.
  • 1863 - Nikola Tesla brother dies after falling from a horse at age 12.
  • 1870 - Tesla moves to modern-day Carlstadt, Croatia to live with his aunt and uncle where he attends school. His teacher, Martin Sekulić, teaches him math & physics.
  • 1873 - Tesla contracts Cholera and is bedridden and near death for 9 months. His father promises to send him to engineering school if he gets better.
    • 1874 - He leaves for a neighboring town to both get better from Cholera and avoid the compulsory 3 year requirement in the army.
  • 1875 - Tesla is enrolled at the Austrian Polytechnic School in Graz on a Military Border scholarship, but drops out two years later.
  • 1878 - He takes his first job as a draftsman for a local engineering firm in Maribor, Slovenia.
  • 1879 - Tesla's father dies, he is deported from Maribor (not having a residence permit), and he moves back to Gospić to teach at his old school
  • 1884 - Moves to New York City and is immediately hired by Thomas Edison; Quits a year later after payment disputes.
  • 1885 - Starts his own company Tesla Electric Light Company in New Jersey and after completing his design is forced out of his company by investors.
  • 1886 - During the winter he was forced to work as a ditchdigger for $2 a day.
  • 1888 - Tesla sells patent the AC Polyphase System to George Westinghouse for $25,000 in cash, $50,000 in notes and a royalty of $2.50 per horsepower for each motor.
  • 1890 - Tesla developed some of the first neon and fluorescent illumination. Later it begins his lifelong obsession - the wireless transmission of energy.
  • 1891 - Tesla becomes an American citizen; The infamous Tesla Coil was invented.
  • 1892 - Nikola Tesla is elected Vice President of the American Institute of Electrical Engineering, now coined IEEE.
  • 1899 - Tesla observed strange rhythmic pulses on the his very sensitive radio receiver. Reports findings and is criticized.
  • 1901 - He signs over 51 percent interest in his patents and inventions, including future ones, relating to both electric lighting and wireless telegraphy or telephony for a mere $150,000.00.
  • 1903 - Wardenclyffe testing begins.
  • 1906 - Wardenclyffe workers are laid off after J.P. Morgan withdraws support.
  • 1909 - Nobel Prize for physics is shared between Guglielmo Marconi and Karl Ferdinand Braun for their efforts in discovering radio. Tesla is furious and tries to correct the injustice.
  • 1916 - Tesla declares bankruptcy.
  • 1943 - Tesla died alone and penniless at the age of 86
